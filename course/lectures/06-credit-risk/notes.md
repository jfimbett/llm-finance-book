# Lecture 6: LLMs for Credit Risk Analysis

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lecture, students should be able to:

1. Identify the main sources of credit data and the regulatory constraints (GDPR, FCRA, ECOA) governing their use.
2. Fine-tune a pre-trained language model for default prediction and extract calibrated implied probabilities via structured generation.
3. Model household decisions under uncertainty using LLMs grounded in utility theory and bounded rationality.
4. Design persona agents that simulate heterogeneous household financial behaviour and calibrate them against survey data.
5. Evaluate a credit model using AUROC, KS statistic, and Gini coefficient within the SR 11-7 model risk framework.
6. Apply SHAP-based interpretability to produce GDPR-compliant credit decision explanations.
7. Architect and deploy a production credit risk application with monitoring and human-in-the-loop review.
