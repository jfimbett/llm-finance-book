- I like to motivate people first by talking about AI and jobs. But thinking of jobs not as just a standard thing that you do, instead to think of jobs as tasks. A job has different tasks, and one might have different skills, knowledge, or talent to do each one of those tasks. For example, a finance professor has to prepare courses, prepare slides, grade exams, propose new courses, do research, analyse data, write models, hire RAs, supervise RAs, supervise phd students, go to conferences, discuss papers, deal with administrative work for reimbursements, administrative work for the university like directing a amster program, service to the community, like refereeing papers, giving feedback to papers from close colleagues in the profession, apply for grants, write grants, etc. If AI can help you spend less time in some of the tasks where you have no competitive advantage (e.g. dealing with reimbursements, travel expenses, scheduling, data collection in some cases), but i can make you more productive in some other tasks, e.g. now you can actually work in more papers in parallel, or you can review your manuscript a lot better before submitting to a journal, AI will not replace your work, instead you would be more competitive. I want here to cite a paper by Dimitris Ppanikolau and some other coauthors on this, they have a model and some nice empirical evidence.
