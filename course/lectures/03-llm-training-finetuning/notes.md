# Lecture 3: Training and Fine-Tuning Large Language Models

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lecture, students should be able to:

1. Identify the major sources of pre-training data and describe standard preprocessing pipelines.
2. Explain the Chinchilla scaling laws and use them to reason about compute–data trade-offs.
3. Contrast causal and masked language modelling objectives and select the right one for a task.
4. Describe distributed training strategies (data, model, pipeline parallelism) at a conceptual level.
5. Apply parameter-efficient fine-tuning (LoRA, adapters) and articulate when to prefer it over full fine-tuning.
6. Explain instruction tuning and RLHF and their role in aligning model behaviour.
7. Benchmark a finance-specific model and interpret results relative to regulatory expectations.
8. Identify bias sources in financial corpora and apply responsible-AI governance principles.
