# Lecture 12: Explainability and XAI in Finance

## Learning Objectives

After this lecture, students will be able to:
- Explain why explainability is legally and operationally required in regulated finance, citing key frameworks such as the EU AI Act and SR 11-7
- Apply classical XAI methods (SHAP, LIME, attention visualization) to language models used in financial applications
- Describe LLM-native explainability mechanisms including chain-of-thought reasoning and counterfactual explanations
- Analyze real-world case studies of financial explainability in credit scoring, loan denial, and investment recommendations
- Evaluate the quality of explanations using faithfulness, completeness, and plausibility metrics
