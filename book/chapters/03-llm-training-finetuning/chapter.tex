\chapter{Training and Fine-Tuning Large Language Models}
\label{ch:llm-training}

\begin{remark}[Learning Objectives]
After completing this chapter, the reader should be able to:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Describe the major sources of training data for large language models and apply
        standard preprocessing and deduplication pipelines to a raw text corpus.
  \item Explain the Chinchilla scaling laws and use them to estimate the optimal model
        size and token budget for a given compute allocation.
  \item Distinguish causal language modelling from masked language modelling and justify
        the choice of pre-training objective for a given downstream task.
  \item Describe data, model, and pipeline parallelism and explain how each addresses
        memory and compute constraints at scale.
  \item Apply parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods (LoRA, prefix tuning, adapters)
        to a pre-trained model and compare their trade-offs against full fine-tuning.
  \item Explain instruction tuning and reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF)
        and evaluate their impact on model alignment.
  \item Benchmark a fine-tuned model on standard financial NLP tasks and interpret the
        results in the context of regulatory and compliance requirements.
  \item Identify sources of bias in financial corpora and apply mitigation strategies
        consistent with MiFID~II governance requirements.
\end{enumerate}
\end{remark}
