\markboth{About the Author}{About the Author}

\noindent\textbf{Juan F.\ Imbet} is an Assistant Professor of Finance. His
research lies at the intersection of corporate finance, asset pricing, and
computational finance, with two main strands of inquiry: the impact of the
offshore finance industry on corporate policies, and the role of social media
in financial markets and investment decisions.

\medskip
\noindent He is an expert in textual analysis and machine learning applied to
finance, with a focus on building and processing large-scale datasets to study
financial markets and corporate behaviour. His methodological work spans
natural language processing, large-scale optimization, and option-implied
measures of market risk.

\medskip
\noindent Among his recent contributions, he co-authored
\textit{Social Media as a Bank Run Catalyst} (with Cookson, Fox, Gil-Bazo, and
Schiller), which documents that banks with high pre-existing Twitter exposure
lost significantly more market value during the Silicon Valley Bank crisis of
2023. He has also published on the effect of social media on mutual fund flows
and on offshore corporate tax strategies.

\medskip
\noindent He holds a Ph.D.\ in Finance from Universitat Pompeu Fabra and the
Barcelona Graduate School of Economics, an M.Sc.\ in Finance from the Barcelona
Graduate School of Economics, and a double B.Sc.\ in Industrial Engineering and
Economics (with a minor in Mathematics) from Universidad de los Andes,
Bogot\'{a}.
