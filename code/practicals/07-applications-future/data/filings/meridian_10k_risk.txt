Meridian Robotics Inc. — Form 10-K (fiscal year 2025) — Item 1A. Risk Factors (excerpt)

Customer concentration. Two automotive customers together accounted for approximately 41% of
revenue in fiscal 2025. The loss of either, or a slowdown in their capital spending on factory
automation, would materially reduce our revenue and could lead to underutilized manufacturing
capacity.

Component supply. Our collaborative robots depend on high-precision actuators and machine-vision
sensors sourced from a small number of suppliers in Japan and Germany. A shortage of these
components, or export restrictions, could delay shipments and raise our cost of goods sold.

Autonomy regulation. As we expand from factory robots into autonomous warehouse vehicles, our
products become subject to evolving safety and liability rules. New certification requirements
could slow product launches or increase compliance costs, and an accident involving our autonomy
software could expose us to litigation and reputational harm.

Foreign exchange. About 35% of revenue is invoiced in euros and yen. A stronger U.S. dollar
reduces reported revenue and can compress operating margin, which we hedge only in part.
